Screen 02 · Library

Library tab

The browse-everything surface: Books, Series, Narrators, Authors. The redesign pulls the sort/filter/layout model up to a persistent one-tap control bar, replaces the blind library-cycle chip with a real picker sheet, and hands search off to the single unified search surface (D-SEARCH). Nothing from the current 3-tab options sheet is lost — it remains the full-depth editor behind the surface controls.

Teardown — what's wrong today

  • Blind library switcher. The header chip cycles libraries with no preview — with 3+ libraries you tap in the dark and read the result. No book counts, no list.
  • Every sort/filter/layout op costs 3+ taps. One "Filter · Sort · View" button opens a 3-tab sheet for even the most common flips (Title↑↔↓, grid↔list). Cited in the systemic teardown as "buried controls".
  • Search hides the view chips. The inline search pill removes Books/Series/Narrators/Authors while typing — you literally cannot search within series or authors. And it diverges from the pushed /search route (D-SEARCH violation).
  • A-Z rail is conditional trivia. The excellent 28px rail (teardrop preview, haptic ratchet, dimmed empty letters) only appears when sort=Title AND layout=grid. Author sort in list view — nothing.
  • Undiscoverable gestures. Pinch-to-resize columns (2..max) and long-press-to-select have zero visible affordance. Both features effectively don't exist for most users.
  • Books view has NO empty state. A fresh or fully-filtered library renders a silent blank. Groups at least say "Nothing here yet" (D-STATES violation).
  • Selection toolbar is icon soup. Close, count, select-all, mark finished, add to list, add to queue, download — seven unlabeled glyphs in one strip.

Redesign moves

  • Library picker sheet — switcher chip opens a proper sheet: every library listed with book counts and a checkmark. The blind cycle is dead.
  • Persistent control bar — sort chip (tap flips direction, chevron opens the sheet), filter chip with count badge, layout toggle, select button. One tap for the 90% ops; the full Display/Sort/Filter sheet keeps everything, including Random and cover size.
  • Search leaves this screen — the header pill routes to the ONE unified search (D-SEARCH). View chips stay visible always, purely for browsing.
  • A-Z rail everywhere alphabetical — all name sorts, both layouts, Books and Groups. Scroll-top pill co-exists bottom-left.
  • Tiles carry state — progress bar under in-progress covers, finished badge, quick-play chip (D-PLAY). Long-press still selects; a one-time "Pinch to resize" hint pill makes the gesture discoverable, and cover size also lives in the Display tab.
  • Selection gets three entrances — long-press (kept), a Select button in the control bar, and a "Select books" row in the sort sheet. Toolbar icons gain 11px labels + an overflow for the rare ops.
  • All four states designed — skeleton grid, real empty state with switch-library CTA, offline chip with downloaded-only note (D-STATES).

Screen 02 · Search

Unified search

The single search surface (D-SEARCH), pushed from the Home and Library headers. Scope chips cover the four Library views plus Everything; a "Beyond your library" Audible section keeps discovery in the same flow; and the lone "search beyond your library" toggle moves out of its dead-end settings screen into the header gear.

Teardown — what's wrong today

  • Two divergent searches. Library's inline pill (debounced, has clear ×, hides view chips, no Audible results) vs the pushed /search route ("Search books" placeholder, auto-focus, NO clear button, Audible section). Two tools, one job.
  • Errors are swallowed. A failed search renders a silent empty grid — indistinguishable from no results (D-STATES violation).
  • Books-only scope. /search can't find series, authors, or narrators at all; the Library search can't either once chips hide.
  • No memory. No recent searches; every session starts from a blank field.
  • Buried setting. "Search beyond your library" lives alone on its own settings screen — a whole navigation slot for one toggle.
  • Audible rows are inconsistent with owned rows (56px cover, different tag treatments: Request when ReadMeABook is enabled, otherwise a "Not in library" cart tag).

Redesign moves

  • One pushed surface — reached from Home and Library header pills via a shared-element morph. Back returns to wherever you came from; the owning tab stays lit (D-NAV).
  • Scope chips — Everything · Books · Series · Authors · Narrators. Everything shows sectioned results; a scope narrows to one type (D-SEARCH).
  • Clear × and gear — the field always has a clear button; the gear opens inline search settings holding the relocated "Search beyond your library" toggle.
  • Recent searches — last 8 queries, tap to re-run, swipe or × to remove, Clear all (new).
  • Beyond your library, consistent — same row anatomy as owned results; Request tag (ReadMeABook) or Audible tag → the same NotOwnedSheet.
  • All four states — typing skeleton, no-results with a "Search beyond your library?" suggestion when the toggle is off, error with retry, offline searching downloads only (D-STATES).

Screen 02 · Author & Narrator

Author & narrator screens

The group drill-in from Library chips, search, and book-detail credits. Today it's the app's most inconsistent screen: square covers, no sort, no states, no identity. The redesign gives it a hero, series-aware grouping, and the same tile grammar as everywhere else.

Teardown — what's wrong today

  • Square covers. The only screen in the app that ignores the user's cover-aspect setting — an adaptive grid of squares with 1-line titles (D-CONSIST violation).
  • No sort, no actions. Just eyebrow + name + "{n} titles" + grid. You can't order by series, year, or anything.
  • No states. No empty state, no loading design — and the narrator variant filters the entire library client-side, which is slow enough to need one.
  • No identity. An author with 14 books across 3 series renders identically to one with a single title — a flat wall of tiles with no structure.
  • Tab bar goes dark on this pushed route like every other drilldown (D-NAV violation).

Redesign moves

  • Cover aspect = user setting — same 2:3 (or whatever the user chose) as the rest of the app, with progress bars, finished badges, and quick-play chips (D-CONSIST, D-PLAY).
  • Hero header — round avatar, AUTHOR / NARRATED BY eyebrow, name, "14 books · 3 series" count line.
  • Sort chip — Title / Series / Year, same tap-to-flip grammar as the Library control bar.
  • Series-aware grouping (new) — when the author has series, books group under tappable series subheads; standalones follow. Series sort makes this the default reading view.
  • Skeleton + empty states — the narrator's client-side filter gets an honest skeleton pass; empty gets an icon + CTA (D-STATES).
  • Library tab stays lit on this pushed screen (D-NAV).

Screen 02 · Series

Series detail

The strongest screen in the app — its per-row play buttons and segment progress track are patterns the whole redesign exports outward. The changes here are surgical: a tappable author, a demoted mark-finished, a legend for the track, and missing books gathered into their own honest section.

Teardown — what's wrong today

  • The author line is dead. Name shown under the series title, not tappable — the one credit on the screen you can't follow.
  • "Mark series finished" is too loud. A full-width secondary button directly under the Continue CTA — a rare, destructive-ish bulk action visually competing with the screen's primary verb.
  • SegmentTrack has no legend. Filled / partial / red-slash cells encode finished / in-progress / unowned — and nothing on the screen says so.
  • Missing books masquerade as owned. Unowned Audible-roster rows sit inside "In reading order" as dimmed rows with Request/Buy/countdown tags — easy to mistake for owned books in a weird state.
  • Per-row play reads as download-gated. The round play works on owned rows, but offline skeleton siblings look nearly identical to streamable rows — the affordance needs to be unambiguous: every owned row plays.
  • What's already right — CoverGlow, HeroCovers fan with +N chip, stat strip, progress card, Continue CTA, BookRow anatomy, long-press selection, offline downloaded+skeleton browsing. Keep all of it.

Redesign moves

  • Author line is a link — accent color + chevron, pushes the author screen. Library tab stays lit (D-NAV).
  • Mark-series-finished demoted — into the header overflow (and it stays available in the selection toolbar). The Continue CTA stands alone (D-FINISH: one tap + actionable toast when used).
  • Micro-legend under the track — ● finished · ◐ in progress · ⊘ not owned. Eleven points of text that make the best visual in the app readable.
  • "Not in your library (N)" — missing books collapse into their own expandable section below the reading order, with a Request-all hint. Owned list stays pure.
  • Play on every owned row — streaming or downloaded, explicitly. Downloaded rows add the badge; undownloaded rows still play (stream). One tap to audio (D-PLAY).
  • Everything else kept — fan, glow, stat strip, progress card, Continue · Book N, row anatomy, long-press selection, offline behavior.
