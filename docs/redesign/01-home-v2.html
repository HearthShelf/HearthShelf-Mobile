Screen 01 · Home

The in-house teardown

The consultant's Home is a real upgrade: the ~240px hero, the dashboard row, the four states, and "See all" as a pushed screen are all correct and ship as-is. Two things need rework — one is an outright regression.

reviewsScored against the consultant's 01-home.html

The redesign's biggest new idea on Home — a quick-play chip that "swaps the hero" — introduces two problems the v1 didn't have. Everything else is endorsed.

check_circleKept — ship as written

  • Compact 240/190px hero pulls content above the fold. Correct.
  • Dashboard row (Up-next peek + streak) is a genuinely good glance surface.
  • Four designed states replacing the swallowed .catch. Best part of the page.
  • "See all" pushes a real screen. Right call over the 85% sheet.

constructionStill wrong — unaddressed

  • No light-mode Home. D-CONSIST promises reactive theme; the hero's white-on-dark gradient has no light variant. Drawn below.
  • Reach unproven. The hero transport sits at ~mid-screen on a tall phone, not clearly in the thumb arc. We overlay it.
  • Greeting + search-only header wastes the top-right; no way to reach More/profile without leaving Home.

warningRegressed — the redesign made it worse

  • Quick-play "swaps the hero." Tapping a shelf chip silently reorders the whole screen's anchor — disorienting, and undoable only by hunting.
  • Silent audio start. A single tap starting out-loud playback with no volume/route confirmation is a public-embarrassment risk (bus, office).
  • Chip vs tile target collision. A 32px play chip on a 104px tile that ALSO opens detail on body-tap invites mis-taps at the corner.
Screen 01 · Home

Quick-play without hijacking the hero — and a header that earns its pixels

Keep the one-tap-to-audio doctrine (D-PLAY) — just don't let it silently rearrange Home or blast audio. In v2 the quick-play chip resumes into the mini dock with an explicit "now playing" state; the hero stays the stable anchor it should be, and the first out-loud second is protected. Revised after review: my first pass "fixed" the greeting + search-only header by dropping in a profile icon — that was a non-answer. The two frames below show the real fix (a priority-ranked status line) and the alternative that honors the current app's "bleed the hero up" instinct at 44px instead of 340px.

u_turn_leftRevised recommendation — the header

You were right, and so was the current app's instinct. Bleeding the hero up to "waste nothing" is the correct value; a 340px hero was the wrong price. So the fix isn't "add an icon" (my non-answer) or "bleed the hero up" (the old mistake) — it's make the top strip carry state and a resume path. My recommendation: ship Option A (status line) as the default, and layer Option B (pin-on-scroll resume bar) on top of it — they compose: A informs at rest, B keeps Resume + search one tap while browsing. Together they beat both the consultant's calm-but-empty header and the current app's useful-but-oversized one.

Home — after quick-play v2 fix
Tapping a shelf tile's play chip starts the book in the mini dock (bottom, thumb zone) with an equalizer "playing" glyph — the hero does NOT swap. The dock is the ambassador; the tapped tile shows a playing ring so you can see what you started.
