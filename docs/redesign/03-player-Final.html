Rejected from the reviews

  • v2's swipe-down-to-dismiss handle — we keep the pure-tab player. The player IS the Now tab; you leave via the tab bar, not by dismissing a modal. There is no collapse chevron and no swipe-down.
  • v2's phone-side "Driving" car lock with a display-only scrubber — the car UI is native (Android Auto / CarPlay own the head-unit screen). The phone does not replicate a driving surface; it only reports "Playing in your car" as a status.

Adopted from the reviews

  • Cover single-tap defaults to inspect, not play v2 — matches the shipping default (tapArtworkTogglesPlay: false). Play-on-cover stays an opt-in setting, off by default, so a stray tap never pauses your book.
  • Immersive → Focus view, kept distinct from native Car mode v3 + user — the phone's big-artwork minimal layout is a personal preference called Focus view; the vehicle experience is separate and native.
  • Accessibility + reduce-motion floor both — VoiceOver/TalkBack labels on every icon-only control, shape-not-color-alone status glyphs, and static fallbacks for every animation.
Screen 03 · Player

Full player

The screen users hold longer than any other. Today its two most-used utilities — queue and sync — sit at the very top, the cover's single tap has no visible affordance, and a chapterless book silently loses its skip buttons. The redesign keeps every feature where it earns its place and drops the frequent controls into the thumb zone (D-THUMB), gives the cover a clear single-tap purpose (inspect), and closes the two shipped bugs.

Teardown — what's wrong today

  • Queue is a bare icon that tells you nothing — today's top-right queue button carries no state; you tap it just to find out whether anything is queued. (FINAL keeps queue in the header — vertical space for artwork outranks thumb reach here — but as a status chip that shows count + mode at a glance, so most checks need zero taps.)
  • Sync sits top-right too, but it isn't an action — it's a passive status indicator (synced / pending / offline) that only occasionally needs a tap. It belongs with the chrome, not among the buttons you press to do things.
  • Skip buttons vanish on chapterless books — skip-back/forward render inside the hasChapters branch, so a book with no chapter track loses its most basic transport. A shipped bug, not a design choice.
  • Cover single-tap has no affordance — the largest tap target on the screen opens the lightbox (inspect), but nothing on screen tells you so; the margin skip hotspots have zero affordance either. tapArtworkTogglesPlay defaults OFF in prod, and that default is correct — play-on-cover is a hair-trigger; the fix is an affordance, not flipping the default.
  • No buffering state anywhere — a stalled stream looks identical to playing; users tap play twice and skip themselves around (D-STATES).
  • A permanent "swipe up for immersive" caption eats real estate — a standing hint band at the bottom shrinks the artwork and buttons to explain a gesture. A one-time thing does not deserve permanent screen space; and the big-artwork view has no visible tap entry at all, only the undiscoverable swipe.
  • Stacked meta rows squeeze the artwork — an earlier FINAL draft stacked title block + action row + a dedicated "Up next" line + carousel dots under the transport; each row stole height from the cover.
  • Whole-book strip is fine but orphaned — the 2 px bar + mono strip (elapsed · bold % · −remaining) works; it just floats under a header crowded with icons.

Redesign moves

  • One row of buttons under the transport — nothing else — the layout law of this screen: scrubber above the transport, ONE configurable action row below it, and the artwork gets every other pixel. Queue status becomes a compact header chip (count + mode, tap = Queue sheet) instead of its own line; the carousel dots ride up under the whole-book strip. No stacked control rows squeezing the cover.
  • The player is the "Now" tab, not a modal — the tab bar stays visible and there's no collapse chevron. As long as the menu is there, "collapse to mini player" is redundant: you leave by tapping another tab (the mini player takes over there), and return by tapping Now. This frees the whole header-left and removes a control that duplicated the tab bar.
  • Sync stays a status indicator, in the header — right side, next to the Focus-view entry. Both are chrome, and sync is never mistaken for an action button. Its full grammar (green synced / accent pending / red offline) and tap-to-open-sheet are unchanged.
  • Skip-back/forward are unconditional — chapter prev/next appear only when chapters exist, at the outer transport positions. Chapterless books keep a full transport (bug fix).
  • Cover single-tap inspects (opens the lightbox) by default — matching the shipping default. Play-on-cover stays a setting for those who want it, but it's OFF by default so a stray tap never pauses your book. A subtle "tap to inspect" affordance teaches it; the margin skip hotspots keep working with one-time hint arcs on first open (D-CONSIST).
  • Chapter position stays in the scrubber — the ember pill already reads "Ch 12 · 33:12" mid-drag; no extra chapter pill above it. Chapters open from the action-row button (pre-scrolled to now-playing, per the Chapters sheet).
  • Buffering ring new — thin animated ring around the play button + "Buffering…" micro-caption (D-STATES).
  • No standing "swipe up" caption — that permanent hint text (and the empty band it justified) is gone. The reclaimed height goes into larger artwork and bigger transport buttons. Focus view gets an explicit tap entry (a small focus icon in the header, next to sync); the swipe-up gesture is taught once by an overlaid first-run tutorial, not by permanent on-screen chrome.
  • Accessibility & reduce motion D-A11Y — every icon-only control carries a VoiceOver/TalkBack label in a logical focus order; the sync indicator uses distinct cloud SHAPES (done / syncing / off), never color alone; and Focus view, EmberBurst, scrubber glow, and carousel parallax all honor Reduce Motion with static fallbacks (buffering shown as text, not just a ring).
  • Everything else is kept — bg options, bookmark + club cover overlays, timeline marker dots, the ember Scrubber, the configurable 6-slot action row with More tray, sleep countdown wash.

Focus view (phone) — and the car boundary

Two different things the v1 suite wrongly fused: the swipe-up minimal layout is a phone preference — big artwork, hidden chrome, for people who want the stripped-back look (some do, some don't; it stays opt-in). The car experience is separate and native: Android Auto / CarPlay own the vehicle screen, and the phone only reports "playing in your car." The action row's "Car mode — coming soon" stub dies; its slot becomes the honest Focus-view entry.

Teardown — what's wrong today (and in v1)

  • Two names, one of them fake — the swipe-up immersive layout works today, while the carMode action button toasts "coming soon"; users who tap it are told the feature doesn't exist while it's one gesture away (D-CONSIST).
  • v1's fix went the wrong way — it fused them into one "Car mode", conflating a phone display preference with real vehicle integration. Android Auto / CarPlay already own the car screen natively (autoBridge.ts + the native MediaLibraryService); the phone must not pretend to be the driving UI.
  • Exit is gesture-only — swipe down works, but an undiscoverable gesture is the wrong only-exit for a chrome-less screen.
  • Good bones — hidden chrome, bigger cover, centered transport and the second chapter row are already right for glanced-at use.

FINAL moves

  • Rename: Focus view — the minimal layout is a phone preference, not a car feature. It hides a lot; some want that, some don't — so it stays strictly opt-in (swipe-up or the header button), never automatic.
  • The car stays native — Android Auto / CarPlay render the vehicle screen. When a head unit connects, the phone shows a small "Playing in your car" status chip; it does not switch layouts or become a driving surface.
  • The stub dies honestly — the "Car mode — coming soon" action is removed; its slot becomes the Focus-view entry that actually works.
  • Glance-first sizing kept — 120px play, 88px skips, 34px title, 40px scrubber; chapter row promoted; sleep + speed pills reachable; visible X exit alongside swipe-down.

Cover carousel & mini player

The carousel (default on) pages the player through the up-next deck; the mini player is the app-wide dock above the tab bar. Both work — both hide their best tricks. The carousel gives no visual clue a second page exists; the mini player wastes its swipe surface.

Teardown — what's wrong today

  • Carousel is invisible — page 0 fills the width edge-to-edge; nothing hints that up-next pages exist. The dots render but read as decoration (undiscoverable gesture — the systemic teardown's #7).
  • Up-next pages are good — dimmed cover, "UP NEXT" kicker, Play-this button, "Back to now playing" chip; switching reorders the deck. All kept.
  • Mini player is display-only beyond its 3 buttons — cover + title + time, skip-back / play / skip-fwd, tap-to-open. The whole row ignores horizontal swipe.
  • Mini progress is a 2px hairline — the top-edge bar is easy to miss over busy screens.

Redesign moves

  • 16px peek new — the next book's cover peeks at the screen edge on every carousel page; the deck advertises itself (fixes the affordance gap).
  • Progress ring on the mini cover new — chapter progress as a ring around the artwork; the 2px bar stays for whole-book.
  • Mini swipe = skip new — horizontal swipe on the mini row skips back/forward with a feedback bloom; tap still opens the player (D-THUMB: the dock is the thumb zone).
  • Everything else kept — dots, Play-this, back-to-now chip, deck reorder on switch, tap-to-open.

Sheets I — chapters, speed, sleep

The listening-control sheets. Chapters can't find "now" in a 50-chapter book; sleep's preset pills look armed but aren't — the single worst trap in the app (users walk away thinking the timer runs). Speed is already good and mostly keeps.

Teardown — what's wrong today

  • Chapters sheet opens at the top — no scroll-to-current, no search; finding chapter 41 of 60 is a scroll safari. The row anatomy (now-playing icon / green check / index + title + duration, tap = seek + dismiss) is fine.
  • Sleep presets don't arm — tapping "30 m" only moves the slider; without an explicit Start nothing happens. Shipped bug per the systemic teardown (#10).
  • No end-of-chapter quick action — the most natural audiobook sleep boundary hides inside the Chapter segment's sub-list.
  • Speed sheet is good — big label, 0.5–3.0 slider step .05 with ticks, presets 0.75/1/1.5/2. Only gap: no fast way home to 1× once you've wandered.
  • Sleep running mode is good — kind label, 68px numeral, ends-at, +5/+10/+15/+30 pills, cancel. Kept whole.

Redesign moves

  • Chapters auto-scroll to current, centered new — plus a search field that appears for books with 50+ chapters. Finished chapters keep the green check (D-STATES: the list shows where you are, not just what exists).
  • Sleep presets arm instantly — tap "30 m" → timer starts, sheet flips to running mode, toast "Sleeping in 30 min", haptic success (bug fix). Start stays for slider/custom/chapter/time values.
  • "End of chapter" pill in the presets row new — one tap arms the chapter-boundary timer without switching segments.
  • "Reset 1×" ghost chip on speed new — appears whenever rate ≠ 1.0.
Chapters sheet redesigned
