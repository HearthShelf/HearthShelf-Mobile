Screen 07 · Sign-in & System

The in-house teardown

The consultant's sign-in rework is strong: bottom-aligned auth block, keyboard-avoidance, real 2FA and forgot-password, theme tokens instead of hardcoded hex, and a serious ember-performance plan. The real gap — and my original framing of it was wrong, corrected after reading prod — is not that "light mode doesn't exist." It does: prod ships a full LIGHT_BASE palette (theme.ts:123), OS-reactive via ThemeProvider.tsx:30. The actual finding is narrower and stronger: sign-in and the splash are hardcoded pre-auth screens that never consume the app's existing light theme.

Two frames the suite owed us: the light-mode sign-in (dimmed art + warm vignette + AA-legible auth block) and the reduced-motion ember fallback (a static, painted glow — no animated particles). The wordmark stays brand-locked in both.
