Read this first

The whole idea in one line

On a phone the player is a stack — everything the app wants to say is queued down one narrow column, so chapters, queue, recent and notes all live behind sheets that cover the art. Unfold the device and that constraint evaporates: the player keeps the left half and every one of those sheets becomes a persistent right-half panel that sits open beside the art instead of on top of it. This is exactly the move the web app already makes on a wide screen (PlayerPage.tsx — .player-col + a .p-panel that slides open, shrinking the cover 320→248px); the fold build brings it to the phone.

The new problem a foldable introduces is the hinge. There is a real crease down the physical center — a slight optical valley and a spot where touch is least reliable and hardest to reach in a two-handed book grip. So the governing rule here is: the seam is a gutter, never a target. The two panes butt up to a ~52px dead zone that straddles the crease; nothing you must tap lives inside it. That single decision is what answers the "play button on the seam" question below — spoiler: no, and the mock shows why.

Everything from the FINAL phone suite is preserved — pure-tab player, one-tap finish, quick-play, the action grammar, the accessibility floor. It is relocated, not redrawn: the bottom tab bar becomes a left navigation rail, sheets become panels, and the folded cover screen simply is the FINAL phone player, unchanged, so the two postures stay one product.

The question you asked

Should the play button live on the seam?

You floated putting the play button on the hinge and weren't sure how it'd feel. I built it both ways. Here's the honest read — see the Player page for the full side-by-side.

blockVerdict — play button ON the seam: no

It's visually gorgeous — the crease reads as the spine of a book and a big round button dead-center feels like the obvious anchor. But it loses on the three things that actually matter for a transport control:

1 · Reach. Hold an unfolded Fold like a book and your thumbs rest at the outer bottom corners, not the middle. The exact center is the single hardest pixel to reach with either thumb — you have to lift a hand off the frame. The most-tapped control should be the easiest reach, which is the opposite corner.

2 · The crease is a bad touch target. The hinge valley is where the digitizer is least flat and where a fingertip rocks over an edge. Pause-at-a-red-light or grab-a-skip should never depend on the least reliable strip of glass.

3 · It fights the gutter rule. Every other element respectfully clears the seam; a button planted on it is the one exception, which makes the crease feel like a defect the design ran into rather than one it designed around.

check_circleWhat ships instead — transport stays in the left palm

Primary: the play/pause + skips live in the left pane, low and centered within that half — right under the left thumb, exactly where the phone player already trains it. The right pane is glanceable content (chapters / queue), which needs reading, not rapid tapping, so it tolerates the longer reach.

The seam still earns its keep, just not as a button: a hairline "now-playing" progress thread runs down the crease itself — a purely ambient spine that shows book progress without ever asking for a touch. The metaphor lands; the ergonomics don't suffer.

Flex / tabletop bonus: when the Fold is half-open on a table, the crease becomes a horizontal fold — art on the top panel, a fat transport bar on the bottom panel, laptop-style. That is the one posture where a centered control makes sense, because the bottom half is now a flat, reachable "keyboard deck." Shown on the Player page.

Fold doctrine

The rules this build adds

The eight phone doctrines still hold. These five are the fold-only additions — cite them in the page teardowns.

F-SEAM

The seam is a gutter, never a target

A ~52px dead zone straddles the crease. No interactive element sits under it. The hinge may carry ambient state (a progress thread) but never a control.

F-PANE

Sheets become panels

Anything the phone opens as a bottom sheet (chapters, queue, recent, notes, sync, details) opens as a persistent right-half panel instead — beside the art, not over it. One at a time; the panel tab strip switches them.

F-RAIL

The tab bar becomes a rail

The five bottom tabs rotate to a left navigation rail with the account avatar pinned bottom. Same five destinations, same lit-owning-tab behavior. No bottom bar on the big screen.

F-CONT

Fold/unfold is lossless

Posture change hands off in place: playback, queue, sleep timer, open panel and scroll position all persist. Fold mid-chapter, keep listening on the cover screen; unfold, the panel you had open is still open.

F-LEFT

Controls left, content right

Frequent controls live in the left pane under the thumb (extends D-THUMB to the horizontal axis). The right pane is for reading and glancing, which tolerates the longer reach across the seam.

The two-pane player

Cover, scrubber and transport hold the left half exactly as they do on the phone. The right half carries the panel that used to be a sheet — here, Up next. Both are live at once: scrub with your left thumb while reading what's queued on the right, no sheet ever covering the art.

Teardown — the phone constraint

  • Sheets cover the art. Chapters, queue, recent, notes all slide up over the cover — you lose the thing you're looking at to see the thing you asked for.
  • One thing at a time. You can read the queue, or scrub, but not both; every panel is a modal round-trip.
  • Wasted width. Unfolded, a phone-shaped player just centers a small column in a sea of black or stretches ugly.

Redesign moves

  • Panels, not sheets (F-PANE). The sheet content becomes a persistent right pane beside the art. Tap strip switches which panel.
  • Transport stays left (F-LEFT). All rapid controls under the left thumb; the right pane is for reading/glancing.
  • Seam is a gutter (F-SEAM). Panes butt to a dead zone over the crease; the hinge carries only an ambient progress thread.
  • Rail, not tab bar (F-RAIL). Five destinations on the left spine-away edge, Now lit.

Two-pane home

The phone home is a tall scroll of rails you dive out of into detail screens. Unfolded, the rails keep the left half and a quick-look opens in the right — same book-actions grammar (D-ACTIONS), no navigation round-trip. Nothing is lost; a tap that used to push /book/:id now just fills the right pane.

Teardown — phone home

  • Every inspect is a trip. Tap a cover → full-screen book page → back. You lose your place on the shelf.
  • Quick-play is hidden behind long-press or the pushed page.
  • Massive unused width when unfolded if you just center the phone column.

Redesign moves

  • Quick-look pane (F-PANE). Tap a cover → right pane fills with cover, blurb, progress, chapters peek and the full action set. Shelf stays put.
  • Rails widen, not multiply. Bigger tiles, same rails (Continue · Recent · Up next · Shelves), scannable two-up.
  • Quick-play on every tile. The play chip is right there (D-PLAY).

Screen 03 · Library & Detail

Master-detail library

On the phone, Library and Book are two separate screens joined by a push/pop. Unfolded they collapse into one: the grid on the left, the selected book's full detail on the right. This is the single biggest reason to reach for the big screen — and it merges what were two of the FINAL suite's pages (02 Library + 04 Book) into one surface without dropping anything.

Teardown — phone flow

  • Pick, push, pop, repeat. Comparing three books means three full navigations and three memory resets.
  • Scroll position is fragile — deep in an A-Z grid, one detail visit and you're hunting for your spot again.
  • Filters hide the grid. The filter sheet covers the very results you're filtering.

Redesign moves

  • Detail is a pane (F-PANE), not a page. Selecting a cover fills the right half; the grid never moves.
  • Filters are a left-pane header, always visible above the results they act on.
  • A-Z rail returns in list view only (FINAL note), riding the left pane's inner edge — clear of the seam.
  • Everything from the Book page survives — series strip, chapters, reader entry, finish, actions — inside the detail pane.
