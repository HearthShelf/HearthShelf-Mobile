What this review is

The consultant delivered a thorough, feature-complete teardown and redesign. We are not throwing it out — most of it ships. This review layer exists because a redesign this large introduces its own problems, and a few of the consultant's "improvements" trade one usability cost for another. Each v2 page sits beside its v1, carries an in-house teardown block (three columns below), and re-draws only the frames that need it.

Doctrine — the in-house addendum

Two rules the redesign is missing

The consultant's eight doctrines stand. These two close the gaps that a dark-only, gesture-heavy mockup suite couldn't. Every v2 page cites them.

D-REACH · extends D-THUMB

Prove the thumb zone, don't assert it

Every primary action must sit inside the bottom ~55% one-handed arc on a 6.7" phone, shown with a reach overlay. Destructive or irreversible actions sit OUTSIDE the fast-tap zone so they can't be fired by a stray thumb. "Reachable" is a measurement, not a claim.

D-A11Y · new

An accessibility & theme floor on every surface

Light mode already ships in prod (theme.ts LIGHT_BASE, OS-reactive) — the job is closing the surfaces that ignore it (sign-in, splash). Text respects Dynamic Type / font scaling up to 200% without clipping. Every icon-only control has a label for VoiceOver/TalkBack, in a logical focus order. EmberBurst, CoverGlow, and carousel parallax all honor Reduce Motion with a static fallback. Color never carries meaning alone (sync state gets an icon shape, not just red/green).
