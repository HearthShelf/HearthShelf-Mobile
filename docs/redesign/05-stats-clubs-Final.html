Reconciliation · v2 + v3 → FINAL

What the two reviews agreed on, and where they were wrong

Both reviews converged on the same target: the heatmap. It is the one card in the tab with a real accessibility gap — 12px squares no screen reader can read and no keyboard/touch user can hit. This FINAL page keeps the consultant Stats and Clubs layout intact and folds in the heatmap work both reviews wanted: a ~48px week-column tap target, a "View as list" dated-rows path a screen reader reads linearly, and the month/weekday axes that prod computes but never draws. Clubs ship exactly as the consultant designed them.

Rejected from the reviews

  • v2's "12px tappable-cell regression." INVALID — the prod heatmap is non-interactive; tap-a-day is the consultant's NEW feature. FINAL makes its target ~48px (the whole week column) so it is genuinely usable, not a regression.
  • v3's "keep Stats and Clubs entirely as-is." Too conservative — the heatmap accessibility gap is real and neither a screen reader nor a fat finger can use a 12px cell. That one card needed the work.

Adopted from the reviews

  • Heatmap "View as list" dated rows + ~48px week-column target + screen-reader table. Both reviews' pick — a linear, non-visual equivalent to the grid plus a touch target a person can actually land on.
  • Month + weekday axis render-bug fix. prod computes monthCols at stats.tsx:948 and never renders them; the grid finally reads as a calendar.
  • Rank and sync never carry meaning by color alone (D-A11Y). Rank change gets an arrow shape, self/sync states get an icon, so color-blind users aren't excluded.
Screen 05 · Stats

Your stats, navigable

The Insights tab is one long scroll of good widgets with no map and no way to refresh. The redesign keeps every card — hero total, tiles, goal, highlights, charts, compare, leaderboard — and adds pull-to-refresh plus a sticky section-jump chip row so a nine-card page stops feeling like a corridor.

Teardown — what's wrong today

  • No pull-to-refresh — the only recovery path is the full-screen error retry; stale numbers sit until you leave and come back (D-STATES).
  • Nine cards, no map — hero, 7 tiles, goal, highlights, bar chart, heatmap, months, compare, leaderboard stack with no way to jump; the leaderboard is ~6 screens down.
  • Orphan tile — the 2-col StatTiles grid leaves the last tile full-width on odd counts (7 tiles today), reading as accidental emphasis.
  • Transient history failure silently drops sections — if the listening-history call fails, the heatmap and month cards simply vanish with no explanation or retry.
  • Streak flame is the only tile with personality — Today's tile buries the most actionable number (today's minutes) at the bottom of the grid.

Redesign moves

  • Pull-to-refresh on the whole tab (D-STATES) — accent spinner, silent re-fetch of stats + history + compare.
  • Sticky jump chips Overview · Goal · Charts · Compare · Leaderboard pin under the header after the hero scrolls away; tap scroll-animates to the anchor.
  • Orphan tile fixed — on odd counts the last tile spans both columns as a horizontal stat strip (value left, label right), reading as deliberate.
  • Hero, tiles, highlights kept — the mono H/M split and the streak flame were already right; they stay.
  • Partial failure gets an inline retry card where the missing section would be — see the states board below.
  • Reduce Motion floor — Reduce Motion makes the goal ring fill instantly (no sweep), the streak/goal EmberBurst a single static frame, and the race pulse a static dot; the numbers are identical, only the animation is dropped.

Charts that answer back & the goal ring

The bar chart and month card were already good — kept. The heatmap was broken and inaccessible: its month labels are computed but never rendered (no time axis at all), its cells are 12px squares no finger can hit and no screen reader can read, and there is no non-visual equivalent. Teardown — what's wrong today

  • Heatmap has no time axis — month labels are computed in code (stats.tsx:948) but never rendered; 26 columns of squares float with zero orientation.
  • Heatmap cells are an impossible target — a 12px cell can't be tapped reliably and a screen reader has nothing linear to read; the wall of orange is meaningless to non-visual and motor-impaired users.
  • Goal reached is anticlimactic — the ring closes to 100% and… nothing. The one earned moment in the tab passes silently.
  • Bar chart hottest-bar accent is good but the Seg (Last 7 / Total / Average) swaps data with no transition — bars teleport.

Redesign moves

  • ~48px week-column tap target — the whole week column (not a 12px cell) is tappable and highlights; the tap-a-day feature the consultant added finally has a target a person can land on.
  • "View as list" dated rows — a toggle switches the heatmap to seven dated day rows ("Mon 09 — 42 min · 2 chapters", "Tue 10 — No listening") that a screen reader reads linearly. The accessible, non-visual equivalent the grid lacks.
  • Month axis + weekday rail rendered — the labels prod computes-and-drops (stats.tsx:948) finally draw: months across the top, S M T W T F S down the left. Render-bug fixed.
  • Selected-week detail card below the grid — the week's total sits in a card under the grid, not a bubble under your finger.
  • EmberBurst on goal completion — the ring sweep ends in a particle burst + "Goal reached" caption swap; happens once per goal, replays on tap. Static single frame under Reduce Motion.
  • Bar chart + month card kept — Seg toggle animates bar heights instead of swapping instantly; 12-month bars unchanged.

Comparing against the server average and friends is the tab's social hook, but the chip row has no overflow plan and a failed history fetch silently amputates two sections. The leaderboard was already strong — kept, with rank change and self-state carried by shape, never color alone.

Screen 05 · Clubs

The club room

The room already does a lot right — the progress race, the spoiler-gated thread, the owner queue. Three things are implicit that should be explicit: who is listening right now (a 10px unlabeled dot), where the new notes start (server cursor, no divider), and whether your note will carry a timestamp (placeholder text only, no control).

Teardown — what's wrong today

  • Timestamp behavior is implicit — notes get a "Chapter X · H:MM:SS" stamp only if you're playing this exact book; the sole hint is placeholder text. No way to see or remove the stamp before sending.
  • "Listening now" is a 10px pulse dot — subtle, unlabeled; most members will never decode it.
  • No unread divider — the server tracks your cursor but the thread renders as one undifferentiated scroll; you hunt for where you left off.
  • Archive is styled destructive-red in the overflow — but archiving is reversible. Color grammar lies (D-CONSIST).
  • Polls every 15s with no visible freshness signal.

Redesign moves

  • Timestamp chip above the composer — "⏱ Ch 12 · 4:32 ✕" shows exactly what will be attached; tapping ✕ sends without a stamp. Implicit becomes a control.
  • "listening now" caption joins the pulse dot on race rows — label plus signal.
  • Unread divider "— new since last visit —" drawn at the cursor; scrolling past it clears the list badge.
  • Archive recolored neutral with a "can be restored" caption; Delete alone stays destructive (D-CONSIST).
  • Everything else kept — race bars, owner queue with Start-promote, spoiler teaser, Reply, SafeSwitch, visibility toggle, deep-link scroll+flash.

Sheets, the ClubCard & the note toast

Redesign moves

  • Archive → neutral row + "can be restored" caption; Delete alone keeps the red and gains a confirm step.
  • Members sheet kept — plus the same "listening now" caption grammar as the race.
  • ClubCard kept with annotations — row order now: reading-this → joinable → owned-elsewhere → create.
  • PopToast kept — annotated; it stacks above the mini dock per the shell's chrome-stack rule.
