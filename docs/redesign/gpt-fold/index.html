Foldable design thesis

The screen opens like the stories inside it.

The fold is not dead space to hide or a centerline to decorate. It is a book spine: focus lives on one leaf, context on the other, and every frequent action remains comfortably away from the hinge.

01Leaf ownershipSelection or playback on the left; detail, lists, and editing on the right.
02Seam-safe controlsNo touch target enters the hinge plus 24 px safety gutter on either side.
03Stable transportOpening Queue or Chapters never shifts the play button under a finger.
04Posture-awareFlat-open uses side-by-side leaves; tabletop uses artwork above and controls below.
Screen 01 · Home

A listening desk, not a stretched feed.

The left leaf answers “what should I continue?” The right leaf gives discovery, clubs, and recent additions room to breathe. The FINAL compact hero survives, but becomes a focused continuation workspace.

Screen 03 · Player

Playback never moves. Context comes to it.

The WebApp’s push-aside panel is the right cue, but the foldable can be stricter: the player owns the entire left leaf from the start. Opening a panel only changes the right leaf.

Verdict on the seam: no play button there. Center it in the left leaf. A seam control would be hard to reach, vulnerable to occlusion, and would shift when hinge geometry changes.

Implementation contract

What makes this foldable, not just wide.

These rules should become shared responsive primitives rather than one-off screen math.

Window segmentation firstUse actual display features/hinge bounds when available. Fall back to a centered 18 px visual spine only in the prototype.
Two thresholds, not device namesCompact: single leaf. Expanded: dual pane. Tabletop: horizontal posture. Do not branch on model.
Independent scroll ownershipEach leaf owns its scroll position. Opening or changing the context pane does not jump the focus leaf.
48 dp targetsEvery interactive element meets the Android minimum, with at least 8 dp between adjacent targets.
Focus order follows panesScreen readers and keyboards traverse the focus leaf first, then the context leaf, then persistent navigation.
Reduced motion by constructionPanel changes cross-fade within the right leaf. No whole-screen push, parallax, or hinge-spanning motion is required.
