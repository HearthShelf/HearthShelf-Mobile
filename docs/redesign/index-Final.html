How FINAL was decided

Two in-house reviews landed on top of the consultant suite. v2 (Opus) critiqued the consultant's mockups, then honestly re-audited against the shipping React Native code and withdrew most of its own "regression" findings as invented or overstated. v3 (GPT) was a production-beta audit whose through-line was "the beta already does this — keep it," conservative to the point of walking back genuine UX wins.

FINAL does the reconciliation the right way: every disputed take was checked against prod source directly (file:line below), not taken on either model's word. The good takes — nearly all of them accessibility, a real bug, or a naming distinction — are adopted. The rest are rejected with a reason. Where the two reviews contradict each other, the code broke the tie.

Net: FINAL changes 6 of 7 pages, but lightly — a focused set of ~12 adopted improvements (mostly the accessibility floor both reviews agreed on, plus two real bugs and two naming corrections), and 9 explicit rejections. The consultant's eight doctrines and its grid-actions / pure-tab-player / one-tap-finish / state-coverage work all stand.
