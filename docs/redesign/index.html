The ten cross-cutting problems

The app's individual screens are feature-complete — the pain is systemic. These ten issues repeat across surfaces, and fixing them once fixes them everywhere. Every section page cites these by doctrine ID.

Systemic teardown

  • Two search experiences. Library's inline search and the pushed /search route differ in placeholder, external results, clear button, and back behavior. Users learn two tools for one job.
  • Playing a book costs 2–3 taps almost everywhere. Only the Home hero and Series rows have 1-tap play; every shelf/grid/search tile detours through the detail screen.
  • The tab bar forgets where you are. Every pushed route re-renders the bar with no active tab; re-tapping a tab does nothing. Navigation gives zero location feedback on drilldowns.
  • Buried controls. Library's entire sort/filter/display model sits behind one button → 3-tab sheet. Player queue & sync live at the top of the screen, out of thumb reach. Reset-progress/hide exist only behind long-press on specific shelves.
  • Mandatory interstitial on every finish. The "When did you finish?" prompt gates all mark-finished paths — the common "just now" case pays an extra tap, and bulk flows pay it too.
  • Missing states. Home has no empty or error state; Books grid, Group screens, and search failures render silently blank. Loading is a spinner, never a skeleton.
  • Undiscoverable gestures. Long-press-to-select, pinch-to-resize columns, swipe-up immersive, long-press-for-default-server — none has a visible affordance; one ("Car mode") even ships as a fake coming-soon button while the feature already exists as swipe-up.
  • Inconsistent primitives. Square covers on Group screens vs 2:3 everywhere else; Seg controls for booleans on Community vs toggles elsewhere; three theming systems (reactive, static import, hardcoded hex) — sign-in, toast, and splash ignore the user's theme and accent.
  • Thin and dead settings destinations. A screen for one toggle (Search), a placeholder screen with six disabled rows (Reading), a temp Diagnostics screen, and stub Admin rows — all consuming top-level navigation slots.
  • Real bugs shipped as UX. Skip-back/forward buttons vanish on chapterless books; sleep presets don't arm without Start; heatmap month labels computed but never rendered; the upcoming-book countdown never ticks.

What stays — the good bones

  • The hearth identity. CoverGlow blooms, EmberBurst on finish, the Scrubber's ember pill, TypesetCover fallbacks, the splash — distinctive and worth amplifying, not diluting.
  • Series detail. Per-row play buttons, segment progress track, Continue CTA — the strongest screen in the app; the redesign exports its patterns outward.
  • Tri-state download control (download → cancel → remove) consistent across detail and long-press.
  • Offline resilience. Downloaded books open, play, and browse from cached catalog; optimistic-with-rollback on bookmarks, follows, dismissals.
  • The configurable player action row with on-screen / tray / hidden placement, editable in-context from the player itself — this is the pattern the rest of settings should copy.
  • Self-hiding sections on book detail (no dead taps), spoiler-gated club notes, promise-based singleton prompts.
  • Motion vocabulary already centralized (POP_SPRING, DUR, SpringPressable) — the redesign specs against it.
Redesign doctrine

Eight rules, applied everywhere

Each section page cites these IDs in its teardown and redesign notes.

D-NAV

The tab bar always knows where you are

One dock, five tabs. Pushed routes keep their owning tab lit (book opened from Library lights Library). Re-tap the active tab to scroll to top. Labels 11px minimum. "Now" remains the flagship center player tab.

D-SEARCH

One search

A single search surface with scope chips (Everything · Books · Series · Authors · Narrators) and a "Beyond your library" Audible section. Reached from Home and Library headers. Search settings live in its own header gear.

D-PLAY

One tap to audio

Quick-play chip on every continue/in-progress tile. Tiles show progress and finished badges. Long-press = the same actions sheet, everywhere books appear.

D-STATES

Design the four states

Every screen ships loading (skeleton shimmer), empty (icon + one line + CTA), error (message + retry), and offline (cached content + status chip). No more silent blanks.

D-ACTIONS

One action grammar

The same BookActionsSheet from every surface, same order. The detail screen's overflow carries the full set — reset progress, hide, bookmarks (always visible), recent listens, share, add to list.

D-FINISH

Finish is one tap

Mark finished instantly; an actionable toast offers "Edit date" and "Undo". The date prompt survives for backdating and bulk imports — as an option, not a gate.

D-THUMB

Controls live in the thumb zone

Frequent actions in the bottom half of the screen. The player's queue and sync move from the header down to the transport zone. Headers get lighter, content starts higher.

D-CONSIST

One way to do each thing

Toggles for booleans, segments for enums, chips for presets. One cover aspect (the user's setting) on every surface. Reactive theme everywhere — sign-in, toasts, and splash respect accent and light mode.
