Redesign study 09 / Navigation motion
A different premise

Motion should explain where you went.

Tabs, drill-downs, and Now Playing do not mean the same thing. They should feel related, but they should not be forced through one transition. The player is a persistent destination—not a dismissible sheet.
Lateral

Parent → child routes may move sideways because the direction communicates depth and gives Back a clear reverse.

Stationary

Bottom tabs keep the frame fixed. Content changes; navigation does not travel with it or pretend the tabs are a stack.

Persistent

Now Playing never moves down. No whole-screen downward exit, no sheet physics, and no gesture that contradicts the missing collapse control.

Three philosophies

Try the routes, then test the player.

Each prototype has the same four destinations. Use the controls to compare tab swapping, book drill-in, and player entry. On the player, drag down or press “Test downward swipe”: every option refuses dismissal by design.

Recommendation
Ship Steady Frame.

It gives each navigation relationship the minimum motion needed to explain itself. The result is calmer than one app-wide transition preset and more honest about the player: Now Playing is part of the app’s persistent navigation, not a temporary sheet to throw downward.

RULE 01
Tabs dissolve in place.

Keep the tab bar and mini-player anchored. Crossfade only the content layer in 140ms.

RULE 02
Hierarchy gets direction.

Book, series, club, and settings detail push from the trailing edge; Back reverses it.

RULE 03
Player does not dismiss.

Remove whole-screen downward translation and swallow downward exit gestures. Tabs remain the exit.

RULE 04
Reduced motion is not “none.”

Use a short opacity change so navigation still has feedback without displacement.
